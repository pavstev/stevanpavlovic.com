CRM Integration involves connecting Customer Relationship Management systems with other business applications to create a unified ecosystem. This includes integrating with marketing automation, sales tools, customer service platforms, and other business systems.

## Related Concepts

- **API Integration**: Connecting systems through APIs
- **Data Synchronization**: Keeping data consistent across systems
- **Middleware**: Integration platforms and tools
- **Enterprise Integration**: Connecting multiple business systems
- **Data Migration**: Moving data between systems

## Use Cases

- Integrating CRM with marketing automation platforms
- Connecting CRM with ERP systems
- Integrating customer service platforms
- Connecting e-commerce platforms with CRM
- Integrating analytics and business intelligence tools

## Technologies

- **Integration Platforms**: MuleSoft, Dell Boomi, Zapier
- **API Management**: Kong, Apigee, AWS API Gateway
- **Middleware**: Message queues, service buses
- **Data Integration**: ETL tools, data pipelines
- **Cloud Integration**: iPaaS solutions

## Best Practices

- Use standardized integration patterns and protocols
- Implement proper error handling and retry logic
- Ensure data consistency and integrity
- Monitor integration performance and health
- Plan for scalability and future growth

## Common Pitfalls

- Data inconsistency between integrated systems
- Performance bottlenecks in integration layer
- Security vulnerabilities in data transfer
- Complex debugging in distributed systems
- Not considering future scalability requirements
